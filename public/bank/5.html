Program with JavaScript Operators, Methods, and Keywords
SubObjective:

Complete or debug code that uses assignment and arithmetic operators

The body of the page contains the following set of code:

<div id="result" class="format-output"></div>
      <script>
           debugger;
           var a = 7, b = 6, c = 13;
           var d = (a & b) | c;
           var e;
           if (d)
                e = d % 2 + (++a - b++);
           document.getElementById("result").innerHTML = e;
      </script>

What will be displayed when the page is previewed in the browser?

3
undefined
2
1

Explanation

e will evaluate to 3. It is important to note the difference between && and || versus & and |. The latter set is used with bits.

To start, evaluate a & b (or 7 & 6). 7 is 0111 and 6 is 0110. 0111 & 0110 will evaluate to 0110 or 6. The result will be ORed with 13. Therefore, 6 | 13 will result in 0110 | 1101 = 1111 or 15. Since d is 15, if(d) evaluates to true, and e will be evaluated.

e = d % 2 + (++a - b++) = 15%2 + (++7 - 6++) = 1 + (8 - 6) = 1 + 2 = 3.

Note that ++7 evaluates to 8, but 6++ evaluates to 6 even if that increments to 7 afterwards.

In this scenario, d is 15 (which is true). If that was not the case (such as 0), then, e will not have been initialized, and "undefined" would be printed on the screen.
